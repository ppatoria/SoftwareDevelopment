1 Low Latency System

1.1 Meaning:

Latency is the time required to perform some action or to produce some result. Data latency may refer to the time between a query and the results arriving at the screen or the time between initiating a transaction that modifies one or more databases and its completion. Latency is measured in units of time – hours, minutes, seconds, nanoseconds or clock periods.

1.2 How to measure:

  1. Latency of HermesAPI: Time taken to process/enrich/convert client request and send/write to socket for Agora.
  2. Latency of Agora: Time taken to process client request and send/write acknowledgment/tisreply to socket.
  3. Latency of Hermes + Agora: Time taken to process/enrich/convert client request and send/write to socket and acknowledgment/tisreply received from Agora.

1.3 What is the latency of your current system ?

Agora: 33 microseconds for 16 instances.

2 High Throughput System

2.1 Meaning:

Throughput is the number of such actions executed or results produced per unit of time. This is measured in units of whatever is being produced (cars, motorcycles, I/O samples, memory words, iterations) per unit of time. The term "memory bandwidth" is sometimes used to specify the throughput of memory systems.

2.2 How to measure:

  1. Throughput of Hermes: Number of messages Hermes process and send to agora per second.
  2. Throughout of Agora: Number of messages agora process and send the reply back to Hermes.
  3. Throughput clients perspective: (a) + (b)

2.3 What is the throughput of your current system ?

30,000~ messages when there are 16 instances.

3 A simple generic example

The following manufacturing example should clarify these two concepts: An assembly line is manufacturing cars. It takes eight hours to manufacture a car and that the factory produces one hundred and twenty cars per day. The latency is: 8 hours. The throughput is: 120 cars / day or 5 cars / hour.
